## 1. Scientific Rationale and Alignment with Boehringer Ingelheim's Mission

Advances in oncology have transformed many cancers into chronic diseases: in Switzerland, more than 60% of patients survive beyond 5 years. This improvement has revealed a critical challenge — the cardiovascular toxicity of anticancer therapies. Cardiotoxicity is the leading non-cancer cause of death in cancer survivors, with the risk of cardiovascular mortality approximately doubled compared to the general population.

Among all cardiovascular risk factors (CVRFs), **diabetes holds a unique and particularly critical position in cardio-oncology:**
- Diabetic patients face a **2 to 3-fold higher risk** of developing cardiac dysfunction under anthracycline therapy
- Approximately **20–30%** of patients evaluated in cardio-oncology clinics carry a diagnosis of diabetes
- Glycaemic control is an independent predictor of cardiac outcomes in cancer patients

SGLT2 inhibitors, pioneered and developed by Boehringer Ingelheim (empagliflozin — Jardiance®), have transformed the management of type 2 diabetes and heart failure. Their pleiotropic cardioprotective mechanisms — **myocardial metabolic reprogramming, mitochondrial protection, anti-inflammatory and anti-fibrotic effects** — make them uniquely positioned as cardioprotective agents in cardio-oncology:
- Preclinical studies demonstrate a **40–70% reduction in cardiac lesions** in animal models exposed to cardiotoxic chemotherapy
- Clinical and meta-analytic data suggest that SGLT2 inhibitor use is associated with a **substantially lower incidence** of cancer therapy-related cardiac dysfunction, reduction in all-cause mortality, and fewer heart failure hospitalisations
- The ESC 2023 heart failure guidelines grant a **Class I, Level A** recommendation for SGLT2 inhibitors in HFpEF — a condition highly prevalent in the cardio-oncology population

Despite this strong evidence base, no Swiss prospective real-world data exist on SGLT2 inhibitor utilisation and outcomes in cardio-oncology. **This project directly addresses that gap.**

## 2. The CVRF–CardioProtect Study

### Study Design
Retrospective and prospective observational cohort of approximately **850–900 patients** evaluated at the CHUV cardio-oncology clinic between January 2025 and December 2026 (approximately 1,000 patients per year, 5–6 per day).

### Primary Objective
To characterise the cardio-oncology population with a specific focus on the **prevalence and control of diabetes** and on cardiovascular pharmacotherapy with particular emphasis on **SGLT2 inhibitor use in patients with type 2 diabetes**.

### Key Diabetes- and SGLT2-Focused Analyses
1. **Prevalence** of diabetes and characterisation of glycaemic control (HbA1c) at baseline
2. **Antidiabetic therapy mapping:** current rates of SGLT2 inhibitor, GLP-1 receptor agonist, and insulin prescription
3. **HFA-PEFF score** assessment: systematic identification of HFpEF patients with Class I indication for SGLT2 inhibitors
4. **Guideline adherence:** proportion of eligible patients who are actually receiving SGLT2 inhibitors
5. **Therapeutic gap quantification:** patients with diabetes and/or HFpEF not yet receiving SGLT2 inhibitors
6. **Longitudinal tracking:** changes in SGLT2 inhibitor prescription rates following cardio-oncology evaluation
7. **Outcome association:** SGLT2 inhibitor use and cardiotoxicity incidence (ESC 2022 definition)

### Primary Endpoint
Cardiotoxicity at 12 months (ESC 2022): LVEF <50% with ≥10% absolute decrease from baseline, or relative GLS reduction >15%.

### Diabetes-Specific Secondary Endpoints
- Change in HbA1c from baseline to 12 months
- Change in SGLT2 inhibitor prescription rates pre- and post-cardio-oncology evaluation
- Association between SGLT2 inhibitor use and cardiotoxicity incidence in diabetic patients
- Rate of de novo SGLT2 inhibitor initiation by the cardio-oncology team

## 6. Conclusion

The CVRF–CardioProtect study will generate **the first prospective Swiss real-world data** on SGLT2 inhibitor utilisation and cardioprotective outcomes in cardio-oncology. This research is directly aligned with Boehringer Ingelheim's pioneering role in SGLT2 inhibitor development and its commitment to improving outcomes for patients with diabetes, heart failure, and cardiorenal disease.

Our findings will:
- Quantify the current therapeutic gap in SGLT2 inhibitor use among eligible cardio-oncology patients
- Provide the scientific foundation for future interventional trials of SGLT2 inhibitors in cardio-oncology
- Inform international guidelines and improve the care of thousands of patients in Switzerland and beyond

We sincerely thank Boehringer Ingelheim for considering this request and for its continued commitment to innovation at the intersection of metabolic and cardiovascular medicine.
